    Canadian Individual Tax Preparation — Complete Course Index¶

    28-Week Professional Training Program for Ontario Tax Preparers

    About This Course

    This course takes a complete beginner from zero knowledge of Canadian tax to a fully operational Ontario tax preparer — ready for their first tax season. Each week builds on the previous. All content is written at expert level with realistic examples throughout.

    Focus: T1 personal income tax returns · Ontario residents · EFILE-certified professional practice

    How to Use This Index¶

    Work through the weeks in order for the first pass. After completing the course, use this index as a quick-reference navigator — jump directly to any topic when a specific client situation arises.

    The most critical weeks for a new preparer, in priority order: 2, 5, 3, 11, 12, 13, 25, 17, 16, 21.

    Phase 1 — Foundations¶

    Weeks 1–4 · The Canadian tax system, the T1 form, and how filing works

    Week Title Core Topics
    Week 1 The Canadian Tax System Tax brackets, marginal vs. effective rate, CRA's role, the T1 flow from income to refund
    Week 2 The T1 Return Structure Every major line number, Schedule 1, Net Income vs. Taxable Income, NOA, worked examples
    Week 3 Filing Basics Deadlines, penalties, SINs, residency rules, EFILE, T1-ADJ, VDP
    Week 4 Phase 1 Review Full mock return, mock client intake, 10-question quiz, Phase 1 readiness checklist

    Phase 1 Priority

    Week 2 is the most important week in the course. Every other week builds on the T1 structure. Master it before proceeding.

    Phase 2 — Income Types¶

    Weeks 5–10 · Every income source a T1 preparer encounters

    Week Title Core Topics
    Week 5 Employment Income T4 box-by-box, taxable benefits, multiple T4s, CPP/EI over-contributions, Form T777, T2200
    Week 6 Investment Income T5, T3, eligible vs. non-eligible dividends, gross-up and DTC, GIC accrual, foreign income
    Week 7 Capital Gains & Losses ACB, Schedule 3, T5008, Principal Residence Exemption, superficial loss, crypto, deemed dispositions
    Week 8 Self-Employment Income T2125, COGS, home office, vehicle logbook, CCA classes, CPP at 11.9%, HST threshold
    Week 9 Rental Income T776, repairs vs. capital, CCA restriction, recapture and terminal loss, co-ownership, basement suites
    Week 10 Phase 2 Review Multi-source income simulation (Victor & Helen Chen), instalment problem, Phase 2 readiness checklist

    Phase 2 Priority

    Week 5 appears on 90%+ of all returns — know it cold. Week 8 (self-employment) generates your highest fees and most complex work.

    Phase 3 — Deductions & Credits¶

    Weeks 11–15 · Every tool that reduces a client's tax bill

    Week Title Core Topics
    Week 11 RRSP — Canada's Most Powerful Tax Tool Deduction limit, bracket strategy, spousal RRSP attribution trap, HBP, LLP, RRIF melt-down, deduction deferral
    Week 12 Common Deductions Union dues, childcare (lower-income spouse rule), moving expenses, carrying charges, advisory fees, support payments
    Week 13 Non-Refundable Tax Credits DTC (retroactive 10-year recovery), medical 12-month window, charitable donations, securities donation strategy, tuition transfers
    Week 14 Refundable Credits & Benefits GST/HST credit, Canada Child Benefit (CCB multiplier), CWB, Ontario Trillium Benefit, FHSA
    Week 15 Provincial Taxes — Ontario Deep Dive ON428, Ontario surtax at $5,315/$6,802, LIFT Credit, OTB components, senior grants, December 31 province rule

    Phase 3 Priority

    Week 11 (RRSP) and Week 13 (credits) contain the highest-value individual client strategies. The DTC retroactive recovery alone can return $18,000+ to a single client. Week 14's CCB multiplier concept — where one RRSP contribution triggers both tax savings and increased benefits — is the most powerful planning insight in the course.

    Phase 4 — Specialized Clients¶

    Weeks 16–21 · Every complex situation you will encounter

    Week Title Core Topics
    Week 16 CRA Audits & Common Mistakes 20 errors that trigger reassessments, review letter response framework, Section 163.2 preparer penalties, engagement letter protection
    Week 17 Seniors, Pension Splitting & Deceased Returns Age Amount, OAS clawback, pension income splitting (T1032), terminal return, RRSP rollover on death, clearance certificate
    Week 18 Students & New Graduates T2202, tuition carry-forward vs. transfer decision, RESP EAP, moving expenses for students, first-employment-year refund
    Week 19 New Immigrants & Non-Residents Part-year residency, 90% test, pre-residency income exclusion, T1135, deemed acquisition on arrival, CCB via RC66
    Week 20 Gig Economy & Side Hustles Uber/rideshare (HST day-one rule), Airbnb classification, Etsy/Shopify COGS, freelancers, content creators, hobby vs. business
    Week 21 Incorporated Clients T4+T5 combined return, salary vs. dividend decision, shareholder loan Section 15(2) trap, LCGE awareness, TOSI flag

    Phase 4 Priority

    Week 17 (seniors) is your highest-fee segment. Week 19 (immigrants) is your fastest-growing segment in Ontario. Week 16 (audits) protects everything you've built. Week 21 (incorporated) delivers your highest single-return fees.

    Phase 5 — Practice & Professionalism¶

    Weeks 22–28 · Running an efficient, legally sound, growing practice

    Week Title Core Topics
    Week 22 Tax Software Mastery EFILE registration, Auto-fill my return (AFR), 8-step workflow, 12-point manual review, ACK numbers, client file system
    Week 23 Setting Up Your Practice Ontario business registration, E&O insurance, engagement letters, PIPEDA privacy, pricing structure, marketing that works
    Week 24 Difficult Clients & Complex Situations PREP framework for bad news, unfiled years, CRA review letters, estate clients, angry clients, prior preparer errors
    Week 25 Year-End Tax Planning The November call, RRSP bracket strategy, tax-loss harvesting (Dec 29 deadline), securities donation (Dec 12), incorporated year-end
    Week 26 Staying Current 5 essential sources, 2-hour monthly system, budget-to-law timeline, referral partner network, complex cases log
    Week 27 Mock Tax Season — Full Simulation 8 complete returns with hidden traps: simple T4, new immigrant, gig worker, senior couple, graduate, rental, incorporated, terminal
    Week 28 Final Readiness Sprint 2025 rate card, pre-season checklist, 28-question self-assessment, four principles of a lasting practice, graduation

    Phase 5 Priority

    Week 25 (year-end planning) is the week that converts you from a filer to an advisor — and builds the client loyalty that sustains a career. Week 27 (simulation) is the most valuable practice exercise in the course. Week 22 (software) is where all your knowledge becomes operational.

    Key Dates at a Glance¶

    Date Event
    January T-slips begin arriving. CRA "What's New" published. Update rate card
    March 1 RRSP contribution deadline (for prior tax year)
    March 31 T3 slips due from mutual funds and ETFs — wait for these before filing investment clients
    April 30 Individual filing deadline. All balances owing due (including self-employed)
    June 15 Self-employed filing deadline (but payment was due April 30)
    July CCB and GST/HST credit recalculated based on prior year returns
    October–November Year-end planning calls with all clients
    December 12 Last safe date to initiate securities donation transfers to charity
    December 29 Last trading day for tax-loss harvesting (T+2 settlement)
    December 31 TFSA contributions, FHSA contributions, charitable donations, dividend declarations
